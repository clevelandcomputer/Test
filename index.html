Honey Run Covered Bridge



(530)891-1838

info@hrcoveredbridge.org		 Honey Run Covered Bridge is a beautifully preserved wooden covered bridge that crosses Butte Creek, in Butte County. Located in the Honey Run Covered Bridge Park which is set in the beautiful Butte Creek Canyon, about halfway in between Chico and Paradise, Honey Run Covered Bridge is managed by the Honey Run Covered Bridge Association and offers a stunning backdrop for picnicking and relaxing. The bridge was built by the American Bridge & Building Company of San Francisco in answer to the need of a route to connect Butte Creek Canyon to the Paradise Ridge after the discovery of gold in Butte County. Honey Run, Covered Bridge Park, is also available for private events such as weddings, anniversaries and other activities and there is a small donation fee for the use of the park located on the site.

670 Honey Run Rd, Chico, CA 95928

Chico Air Museum



(530)345-6468

chicoairmuseum@gmail.com		 The Chico Air Museum is a museum dedicated to aviation, aircraft, and space artifacts. Located at the Chico Municipal Airport, the Chico Air Museum was established to collect, maintain, and exhibit the history of aviation, aircraft, and space artifacts and educate visitors on the importance of the industry. The museum houses a variety of aircraft, artifacts and aviation-related exhibits in a well-maintained World War II-era hangar that was once part of the Chico Army Airfield and located next to the Chico Air Attack Base. The museum also features an outside static display area, an extensive collection of scale models of airplanes, and an extensive aviation research library. The Chico Air Museum is open from Thursday through Sunday.

165 Ryan Ave, Chico, CA 95973

Bidwell Park



(530)895-4711

info@chicorec.com		 Bidwell Park is a public municipal park in Chico that features an array of attractions and points of interest and a nature center. The park spans 11 kilometers in length and boasts 3,670 acres of beautiful landscapes, ranging from unusual rock formations such as the Chico Formation sandstone, thick canopies of trees, and the Chico Municipal Golf Course. The third largest municipal park in California, Bidwell Park is divided into Upper, Middle and Lower sections and points of interest in the park include Sycamore Pool, a beautiful pool built in the late 1920s with concrete decks, walls, and bottom to contain Big Chico Creek that offers a unique swimming experience. Other attractions include Caper Acres Playground, Cedar Grove, Horseshoe Lake, Monkey Face rock formation, Alligator, Bear and Salmon Hole, Diversion Dam, and Devil's Kitchen.

Old Chico Canyon Rd, Chico, CA 95928
